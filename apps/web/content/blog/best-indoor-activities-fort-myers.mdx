## The Attractions at HeadPinz Fort Myers

### 01. Bowling Lanes

Few activities bring families together like bowling, and HeadPinz Fort Myers delivers the full experience. With multiple lanes, bumpers available for younger kids, and a high-energy atmosphere that feels more like a celebration than a chore, a few frames here never get old. Bowling at HeadPinz Fort Myers is genuinely competitive fun — and yes, trash talk is encouraged.

HeadPinz also runs the [Kids Bowl Free](/kids-bowl-free) program during the summer, so families can stretch their fun budget even further.

### 02. GameZone Arcade — Fan Favorite

The GameZone at HeadPinz Fort Myers is not your average handful of aging machines in the corner of a bowling center. This is a massive, dedicated arcade experience with games for every age and play style — from classic ticket-redemption games that send younger kids absolutely wild, to skill-based and competitive setups that hook older kids and teens for hours. Tickets add up fast, and the prize counter delivers real redemption value.

Parents: there's seating, there's food nearby, and the bar is right there. You're welcome.

### 03. Laser Tag Arena

The HeadPinz laser tag arena is one of the best in Southwest Florida, full stop. The multi-level, darkened arena gets the heart rate up and the adrenaline going in a way that younger kids find thrilling and older ones find legitimately competitive. Teams mix up fast, so families end up going head-to-head in the most fun way possible. If your family has a tradition of friendly competition, this is the room where legends are made.

### 04. Ropes Course

For families with kids who love a physical challenge, the indoor ropes course at HeadPinz Fort Myers is a standout attraction. Climbers tackle a multi-level course of bridges, planks, and obstacles — all safely harnessed — while the rest of the family watches and cheers from below (or panics quietly). It builds confidence, burns energy, and gives kids a genuine sense of accomplishment that a video game just can't replicate.

### 05. Full-Service Dining & Bar

Nobody should have to leave a great time just because someone's hungry. HeadPinz Fort Myers has a [full-service restaurant and bar](/menu) on-site, with a menu that covers all the good stuff: pizza, wings, handhelds, and more. Kids get real meal options, adults get cold drinks and a reason to stay a little longer, and the whole family can refuel without ever walking to a parking lot.

The location also makes for a great spot to catch a game, with multiple screens and a sports watch-party atmosphere that feels lively without being overwhelming for families.
