How FastTrax Racing Works

This Isn't Just Go-Karting. This Is Real Racing.

FastTrax is built around a real motorsport-style progression system. Every racer earns their speed. You start in Starter class, prove your skill with qualifying lap times, and unlock faster karts and more challenging tracks as you level up. No shortcuts — that's what makes it exciting.

Leveling up is part of the fun. The progression system means every race matters. Hit your qualifying time and you'll unlock the next tier — faster karts, higher speeds, and bragging rights. Most racers qualify in 1–2 heats. Some do it on their very first race.

How Karts & Timing Work

📋 Your kart number IS your timing identity. The pit board shows your starting position — exit in order. All lap times, race data, and qualification are tied to your kart number.
🟡 Do not pass under yellow flags. Passing under yellow puts you in the wrong kart — your laps won't count correctly toward qualification.
🏁 Up to 14 other drivers on track. Traffic is part of the challenge. Learning to find gaps, time your passes, and work through the pack is what separates racers who qualify from those who don't.
📈 Only your single fastest lap counts. One clean, fast lap is all you need. Most racers qualify in 1–2 heats.
⚡ Tips to Qualify Faster
• Smooth is fast. Avoid sudden braking and sharp turns. Consistent lines beat raw speed every time.
• Race off-peak. Weekday mornings and early afternoons have fewer racers — more room for a clean lap.
• Learn to pass. Getting stuck behind slower drivers? That's racing. Find the gap, be patient, make your move cleanly.
• Use the app. Check your lap times after each heat — your single best lap counts toward qualification.
• Can't quite hit the time? That means there's room to improve your racing line, braking points, and exit speed. Come back and beat your personal best.
On-Track Safety
Minor bumping can happen — it's part of the nature of the track. However, excessive or intentional contact is never allowed and will result in removal from the race.

If you ever feel uncomfortable during your race, safely pull your kart to the side out of the way of other racers, come to a complete stop, and raise your hand. A track marshal will assist you immediately.
