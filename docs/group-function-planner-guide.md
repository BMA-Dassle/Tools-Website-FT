# Group Function Contracts — Event Planner Guide

**Effective:** June 2026  
**For:** Event Planners, Guest Services Managers

---

## What's Changed

We've completely rebuilt our group event contract process. Contracts are now created, signed, and paid entirely through our website — no more PandaDoc. Payments and event status update automatically in BMI Office.

**Why we made this change:** We were seeing too many errors with events arriving on-site — centers changing quotes (mainly lowering them), guests showing up without a form of payment, and manual steps getting missed. The new system closes those gaps by automating the entire process from quote to payment to event day.

---

## The New Process

### 1. You Set Up the Event in BMI Office

Create the event in BMI Office as you normally would. Fill in:

- **Event name** — Use the client's name followed by the event type: "Johnson Birthday Party," "Acme Corp Team Building," "Spring Creek Elementary Field Trip." The system cleans up formatting automatically — you don't need to worry about capitalization or venue prefixes.
- **Date and time**
- **Customer contact** — Name, email, and phone. Email is required — this is how the contract gets delivered.
- **Products and pricing** — Add everything the event includes. This becomes the contract the customer signs.
- **Public notes** — These are what the customer sees. Include arrival time, food timing, lane/activity details, and any special instructions. The system lightly cleans up grammar and spelling before the customer sees them, but won't change any of your details.

When you're ready, set the state to:

- **"New Deposit Requested - HPFM"** (HeadPinz Fort Myers)
- **"New Deposit Requested - FT"** (FastTrax)
- **"New Deposit Requested"** (HeadPinz Naples)

**That's it.** The system takes over from here.

### 2. What Happens Automatically

Within 1-2 minutes of setting the status:

- The customer receives an email and text message with a link to their contract
- You are CC'd on the email
- The contract shows everything you entered: event details, pricing, notes, cancellation policy
- The customer reviews, agrees to the terms, signs (typed or drawn), and pays their deposit

### 3. What the Customer Pays

- **All events require a 50% deposit and a card on file.**
- The remaining 50% is automatically charged 72 hours before the event.
- If the event is less than 96 hours away when the contract is sent, **100% is collected upfront** — there isn't time for the normal deposit/balance cycle.
- The customer receives a receipt email after each charge.

### 4. What Updates in BMI Office

After the customer signs and pays:

- The event status changes to **"Confirmation"** (or **"Confirmation + Waiver"** if the event includes laser tag, racing, or other waiver activities)
- The deposit payment is recorded in BMI Office automatically
- You don't need to update the status or record the payment manually

### 5. Day of the Event

Payment is handled automatically on event day. **No staff action is needed for payment.**

## Important Rules

### Centers Cannot Modify Quotes

**Centers do not have access to change the contract tabs.** The pricing in the contract is locked to what you entered in BMI Office. If the event needs add-ons on the day of (extra guests, additional food, arcade cards, etc.), **the center opens a secondary tab** and collects payment via credit card at the time of service.

The original contract tab and the add-on tab are separate. The contract covers what was agreed to in advance. Anything extra is a new charge.

### The Portal Shows "Paid in Full"

Once the 72-hour balance charge completes, the customer's event shows as paid in full. Staff will see this reflected in Square when the event day arrives.

### All Quotes CC the Event Planner

Every email the customer receives — contract, updates, deposit confirmation, balance receipt, reminders — is CC'd to you. You always know what the customer is seeing.

### All Payments Update Automatically

You do not need to manually record payments in BMI Office. The system records the deposit when the customer pays and updates the status. The 72-hour balance charge is also recorded automatically.

## Making Changes After Sending

### Before the Customer Signs

Update anything in BMI Office — customer info, products, pricing, notes, date. The system checks for changes every 5 minutes and automatically re-sends the contract with updated details.

### After the Customer Signs and Pays

- **Contact info changes** (name, phone, email, planner): Updated automatically, no re-signing needed.
- **Pricing or product changes**: The customer is required to re-sign the contract. They receive a "Contract Updated" email. Their deposit is preserved — they just need to acknowledge the new terms.
- **Notes changes**: Updated automatically and shown on the customer's event page.

### Cancelling an Event

Set the BMI Office state to **"Cancellation."** The system will:

1. Block the contract page — the customer can no longer sign or pay
2. Refund all payments to the customer's card (allow 5-10 business days)
3. Send the customer a cancellation email with refund confirmation
4. No further action needed from you

## Writing Good Event Notes

Your public notes are what the customer sees on their contract page and in the "You're All Set" confirmation after paying. The system cleans up grammar and spelling, but keeps your content exactly as written. A few tips:

**Do:**

- Include arrival time and check-in instructions
- Mention food timing ("Pizzas at 12:00, cake at 1:30")
- Note any special setup ("Check-in table near front entrance")
- Include headcount and lane/activity details
- Mention waiver requirements if applicable

**Don't worry about:**

- Perfect spelling — the system fixes it
- Formatting — the system handles punctuation and capitalization
- Venue name in the title — the system removes "HeadPinz Welcomes" etc.

**Example — Before (what you type):**

> arriving at 10. bowling with 5 kids per lane will adjust based on headcount. pizzas at 10:45 will provide lane assignments. laser tag rotated in during bowling. $5 gamezone cards for students without waivers

**Example — After (what the customer sees):**

> Arriving at 10. Bowling with 5 kids per lane, will adjust based on headcount. Pizzas at 10:45 — will provide lane assignments. Laser tag rotated in during bowling. $5 GameZone cards for students without waivers.

Same information, just cleaned up.

## Customer's 96-Hour Reminder

Four days before the event (24 hours before the balance charge), the customer automatically receives a reminder:

- "Your event is almost here!"
- Verify guest count and event details
- Update their card on file if needed
- Complete waivers (with a direct link, if waivers are required)

You don't need to send any reminders manually.

## Post-Paid Accounts

Some events use post-paid billing (the "GF Post Paid Account" product in BMI). These work differently:

1. The system detects the post-paid product and sends an **approval request** to management (eric@headpinz.com and jacob@headpinz.com)
2. Management reviews the event details and approves or denies
3. **If approved:** The contract is sent to the customer — no deposit is collected
4. **If denied:** You receive an email with the reason. The contract is not sent.

Post-paid events do not go through the automatic payment cycle. Payment collection for post-paid events will be handled separately.

## Troubleshooting

**"My event isn't sending"**

- Make sure the status is set to "New Deposit Requested" (HPFM, FT, or Naples)
- Confirm the customer has an email address in BMI Office
- Wait 1-2 minutes — the system checks the queue frequently

**"The customer says the time is wrong"**

- Check the event time in BMI Office — the contract pulls the time directly from there
- If you fix it in BMI, the system will update the contract within 5 minutes

**"I need to resend the contract link"**

- Change the BMI state away from "New Deposit Requested" and back again. The system will re-send.

**"The customer wants to change their card"**

- They can do this themselves on their "You're All Set" page — there's an "Update Card on File" section. You can also send them the contract link again.

**"I need to cancel after the customer paid"**

- Set BMI state to "Cancellation." The refund is automatic.
